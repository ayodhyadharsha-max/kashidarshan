Ayodhya and Varanasi are the two great poles of a North-Indian pilgrimage — the birthplace of Lord Ram and the eternal city of Lord Shiva. An Ayodhya to Varanasi tour package links them into one smooth journey. Here's how to plan it.

Ayodhya to Varanasi — distance & route

The two cities are about 200–230 km apart (roughly 5–6 hours by road). The most comfortable option for families is a private AC car or tempo traveller, which lets you stop as you please. Trains and buses also run, but a private vehicle saves time and hassle with luggage and elders.

4-day itinerary

  • Day 1 — Arrive Ayodhya, Saryu Aarti in the evening.
  • Day 2 — Ram Janmabhoomi darshan, Hanuman Garhi, Kanak Bhawan; drive towards Varanasi.
  • Day 3 — Kashi Vishwanath darshan, evening Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat.
  • Day 4 — Sunrise boat ride, optional Sarnath, departure.

Kashi Vishwanath darshan

The newly expanded Kashi Vishwanath Dham connects the temple directly to the ghats. We help with darshan timings and, where available, sugam darshan passes so you spend less time in the queue.

Catching the Ganga Aarti

The grand evening Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat draws large crowds. The best ways to see it:

  • Reserved ghat-side seating (we arrange this in advance)
  • From a boat on the river — a serene, uncrowded view
Tip: Reach the ghat by 5:30 pm for a good spot, or take a sunset boat to watch the aarti from the water.

Cost & what's included

A 4-day Ayodhya–Varanasi tour package starts around ₹13,900 per person, including stays, all travel between and within the cities, a guide and darshan assistance.

Frequently asked questions

How far is Varanasi from Ayodhya?

About 200–230 km, roughly 5–6 hours by road in a private vehicle.

How many days for Ayodhya and Varanasi together?

4 days / 3 nights is comfortable. Add a day if you want Prayagraj — see our Ayodhya Prayagraj Varanasi guide.

Can you get Ganga Aarti seats?

Yes — we arrange reserved ghat-side seating or a boat to view the aarti from the river.
