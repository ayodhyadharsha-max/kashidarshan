Best Time to Visit Ayodhya for Ram Mandir Darshan

Travel Tips5 min readUpdated May 2026

In this guide

  2. Festivals to plan around
  3. Avoiding crowds
  4. What to carry
  5. FAQs

The short answer: the best time to visit Ayodhya is October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant for temple-hopping. But the right time also depends on whether you want grand festivals or quiet darshan.

Season by season

  • Winter (Oct–Mar) — Best overall. Pleasant 10–25°C days, ideal for darshan and the Saryu ghats.
  • Summer (Apr–Jun) — Hot (up to 40°C+). Fewer crowds; start early and rest midday.
  • Monsoon (Jul–Sep) — Green and atmospheric, occasional heavy rain; the Sawan month is spiritually special.

Festivals worth planning around

  • Ram Navami (Mar–Apr) — Lord Ram's birthday; the city is electric but very crowded.
  • Deepotsav / Diwali (Oct–Nov) — Lakhs of diyas light the Saryu — breathtaking, book months ahead.
  • Sawan & Kartik Purnima — Auspicious snan days with large gatherings.
Tip: For peaceful darshan, choose a weekday in winter and avoid major festival dates. For atmosphere, time your visit with Deepotsav — but plan early.

Avoiding the heaviest crowds

Weekends, public holidays and festival days see the largest rush. Early-morning darshan (just after opening) is always calmer. We schedule darshan timings to dodge the worst queues and, where available, arrange sugam darshan.

What to carry

  • Comfortable, easily removable footwear (you'll walk barefoot in temples)
  • Light woollens for winter mornings and evenings
  • A small bag for prasad; many temples restrict phones and large bags

Frequently asked questions

What is the best month to visit Ayodhya?

November to February offers the most comfortable weather for darshan.

When is Deepotsav in Ayodhya?

Around Diwali (October–November). It's spectacular but extremely crowded — book well in advance.

Is summer a bad time to visit?

Not bad, just hot. Crowds are thinner; plan early-morning and evening darshan and rest midday.
